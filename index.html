Academic work

My thesis was a literary-historical account of the contemporary poet J.H. Prynne's early career, concentrating on the period between 1955 and 1975. I used archival materials to look closely at Prynne's British influences. I'm also interested in the digital humanities, particularly applications of social network analysis and graph theory to literary history, and have recently published articles in Textual Practice and the Journal of British and Irish Innovative Poetry For more information, see my academic CV and my GitHub profile.

About me

I'm a Data Journalist working for The Times and The Sunday Times. I recently finished a PhD in contemporary poetry at the University of Sussex. Before that, I worked as a technology journalist and completed my undergraduate and masters degrees at the universities of Oxford and Cambridge.
