Journalism

At The Times and The Sunday Times I work on data-driven investigations, with a particular interest in cyber-crime and the dark net. My work has included front-page expos√©s of the black market in stolen credentials, dark net paedophile networks and 'sugar daddy' dating sites, and an investigation naming a government contractor selling hacking equipment on a dark net market. I was the first journalist to provide independent proof of the link between the death of Alexandre Cazes and the shutdown of the dark net market AlphaBay.

Aside from my work on cyber-crime and other original stories, I provide support to reporters and editors working on topics ranging from the royal family to pharmaceutical companies. Most of my work is done in the R programming language.

In 2012 and '13 I worked as a News Writer for The Verge. As a freelancer I've covered Freedom of Information, exam boards and research funding for Times Higher Education, GCSE marking and appeals for The Guardian and produced a report on the postgraduate funding crisis for the Intergenerational Foundation.

Academic work

My doctoral thesis was a literary-historical account of the contemporary poet J.H. Prynne's early career, concentrating on the period between 1955 and 1975. I used archival materials to look closely at Prynne's British influences. I'm also interested in the digital humanities, particularly applications of social network analysis and graph theory to literary history, and have recently published articles in Textual Practice and the Journal of British and Irish Innovative Poetry. For more information, see my academic CV and my GitHub profile.

About me

I'm a Data Journalist working for The Times and The Sunday Times. I finished a PhD in contemporary poetry at the University of Sussex in 2016. Before that, I worked as a technology journalist and completed my undergraduate and masters degrees at the universities of Oxford and Cambridge.
